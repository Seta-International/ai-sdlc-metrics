# **Executive Summary**

SETA — now a 300+ person organization — runs four separate internal applications that are fragmented in data, disconnected in workflows, and invisible to each other. A recent request to build a KPI system has made this pain concrete: we cannot build meaningful KPIs on top of four systems that do not share a common understanding of who our people are, what our projects are, or what our numbers mean.

Rather than simply consolidating data into one internal system, we are building an agent-native enterprise operating system: a unified platform with a canonical data layer and an embedded AI agent ecosystem. We validate it on our own operations first, then bring it to market — targeting the 500,000 Vietnamese SMEs being pushed to digitize by 2030, and scaling globally to the 500 million SMBs facing the same structural problem.

AI development tools have reached a quality threshold where a lean, focused team can deliver this at a pace that would have required 3–5x the headcount two years ago. The bottleneck is not engineering volume — it is architectural clarity and protected focus.

# **Part 1: The Problem and What Changes**

This section is organized by stakeholder — the pain each group experiences today and what the platform replaces it with.

## **For the Board & Leadership**

**Today:** Leadership cannot make decisions from trusted data. When someone asks “what was our margin on last quarter,” the answer takes days and involves spreadsheets nobody fully trusts. Four systems hold pieces of the truth. None hold the whole picture. The KPI system the Aeris Manager requested cannot be built on top of four disconnected databases.

**With the platform:** A single executive dashboard with real-time metrics across all functions. Cross-functional questions answered in seconds from trusted, canonical data. The KPI system draws from one source of truth — not four. The platform is simultaneously solving our operational problems and building a commercial product with a compounding structural moat.

## **For Department Heads & Managers**

**Today:** The HR manager spends two days every month reconciling timekeeping with leave records and payroll in Excel. Contract expirations are tracked in a personal spreadsheet. Vietnamese labor law — PIT brackets, insurance contributions, contract type rules — lives in her head, not in any system. The delivery lead cannot produce a utilization report because data lives in three unconnected places. Finance assembles project profitability quarterly; by the time data is ready, the project is over.

**With the platform:** Payroll reconciliation, leave management, and compliance workflows are automated with human checkpoints. Utilization is real-time. Project profitability is always current. Managers ask questions in plain language and get accurate, sourced answers — no more assembling reports from multiple exports.

## **For All Team Members**

**Today:** Employees message HR on chat to ask about leave balances, payslip details, or policy questions. They wait for someone to manually look it up. Information about projects, assignments, and company policies is scattered across tools and documents.

**With the platform:** A clean, modern interface — on par with Notion or Linear, not a legacy ERP — where any team member can ask questions in plain language and get instant, accurate answers. Agents explain their reasoning and show their sources.

## **For the Engineering & Product Team**

**Today:** The four internal applications solved real problems, but were built independently without a shared data model or identity system. Retrofitting integration is a diminishing-returns exercise — every new connection adds complexity without solving the root cause: there is no canonical layer underneath.

**With the platform:** A greenfield architecture with a clean kernel, typed module SDK, and agent runtime. No legacy debt. Every architectural decision made deliberately. Built with the best AI development tooling available.

# **Part 3: The Platform**

| One-line pitch: _Stop fighting your software. Run your entire organization through one agent-native platform — where your data is trustworthy, your workflows are governed, and your agents actually work._ |
| :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

An Agent-first Enterprise Operating System, delivered as Agent as a Service (AaaS). Organizations subscribe, activate domain modules, and operate through one platform where a beautifully designed UI and AI agents work together. Not a chatbot on existing software — a complete system that replaces the fragmented stack without a six-month ERP implementation.

## **Architecture: Built Bottom-Up**

Most platforms build top-down: UI first, AI second, data third. That is why AI pilots fail. We build bottom-up:

1. **The Process Kernel** — canonical data layer giving every entity (people, projects, accounts, decisions) a single source of truth. Unified identity, explicit authority and delegation, immutable event spine, data quality enforcement.

2. **The Agent Runtime** — governed, sandboxed environment with a 5-layer security model. Agents earn autonomy phase by phase through demonstrated accuracy.

3. **Domain Modules** — seven foundations: HR & People Ops, Workforce Operations, Staffing & Project Ops, Finance Ops, CRM & Engagement, OKR & KPI, and Business Intelligence. Each extends the kernel; none fragment it.

4. **The Channel Layer** — agents deliver through web, mobile, messaging platforms, Slack, MS Teams, and more. Channel-agnostic by design.

## **Agent Phases**

Trust is earned, not declared. Users should expect capability to roll out in four phases:

| Phase                         | What You Will See                                                                                                                               |
| :---------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Phase A:Knowledge & Q\&A      | Agents answer questions over documents, policies, and data. Read-only. Zero risk. Where every user’s trust journey begins.                      |
| Phase B:Data & Insights       | Agents query metrics, generate charts, run summaries. Still read-only. Surfaces operational intelligence leadership has never had in real time. |
| Phase C:Action Proposals      | Agents draft workflows for human review. Nothing executes without explicit approval.                                                            |
| Phase D:Autonomous Monitoring | Agents detect anomalies and propose actions on schedule. Earned only after demonstrated accuracy in earlier phases.                             |
